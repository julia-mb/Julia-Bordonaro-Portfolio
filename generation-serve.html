Case Study
Generation SERVE Website Redesign

Project Overview

Generation SERVE is a Texas-based non-profit whose mission is to engage children in volunteerism and empower them to make a difference in their communities. Our team worked to redesign their mobile and desktop interfaces to be more efficient and interactive. 

The Problem

Generation SERVE is an events-based organization, but the COVID-19 pandemic has caused them to lose 50% of their monthly recurring donations and more than 50% of their staff.

The Solution

Our redesign will streamline processes, and create clear calls to action for users in the site to increase donations and to increase engagement with current and prospective Generation SERVE member families.

The Impact

With our redesigned calendar, we found that users were able to find a specific event 45% faster in our mobile version, and 90% faster in our desktop version.

User Research

Hypothesis

If we improved the site navigation, and expanded the homepage and activities calendar interfaces to include the most relevant information, as well as streamlining the registration process, it would lead to an increase in registrants, and an increase in volunteers, who would then become additional donors.

Initial user research

As part of our research, we:

  • interviewed 4 parents to better understand their main interests and concerns they would have for volunteering with their children
  • conducted 4 usability tests on the current site to determine what pain points users experience when performing tasks within the site
  • interviewed the Executive Director of Generation SERVE to understand the business' needs and goals
Empathy mapping

Following our one-on-one interviews, we created an empathy map to better understand Generation SERVE's potential users and their interests in getting their children involved in volunteering, as well as their interest in becoming involved with an organization like Generation SERVE.
